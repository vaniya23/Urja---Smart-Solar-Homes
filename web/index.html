Solar Panel Subsidy in India, 2023

Let's get to know about government's solar subsidy scheme in India & subsidies of different states of India.

Let's Get Started

Solar Subsidy

What is the meaning of Solar Subsidy?

We know that Solar Energy is the free & clean energy and it reduces the dependency of natural resources, such as Water and Coal. Government wants to install solar power in every house through which each home's roof will produce self-energy. Government supports financially to install solar power in residential homes, thus providing Solar Subsidies.



Why Government gives Solar Subsidy?

India had target to install 40GW solar panels at residentail home buildings, but India has achived only 5GW till 2021. Indian Goverment has set the target of 280GW solar panels by 2030 which means, 10GW solar panels should be installed every year. Government has taken new intitives to install solar panels at homes, such as any customer can install solar panels by any solar dealer / distributors / company, installers and after installing they will have to send solar panel installation photo to the nearest electricity board and apply for solar subsidy.

Subsidy Merits

Customer Benefits

The subsidy is only available for Residential Homes (Individual Homes & Big Apartments), not commercial and industrial sectors. A solar subsidy is only available on Grid Connected Solar System (Without Battery System). As installing a solar system is a huge investment, the financial assistance motivates people to invest and takes some burden off their shoulders.

Homeowners can only install a solar system and claim subsidy through the state DISCOM. They will have to share customer details and register the nearest channel partner. If you install solar system though subsidy scheme, the solar installation company will provide 5 years of performance warranty, then after you are self responsible for your solar system. This subsidy is not available for commercial and industrial customers because they can avail other benefits like accelerated depreciation, tax holidays, excise duty exemptions.


Channel Partner Benefits

Channel Partners benefit from a larger customer base and more business. It is difficult for a common person to figure out the processes of getting the subsidy though they very much desire it. It is now that the channel partner comes into the picture. They try to work with the customer and the government departments and expedite the process of getting a subsidy for the customer.


DISCOMS Benefits

Power consumption demands reduce in residential sectors and they can give more in commercial area. We know that Commercial areas need more electricity than residential. The customers will be happy as they will get their financial aid, the channel partner will be happy as it will get a new customer and the government will be happy too as it will get one step closer to its solar panel installation target.

Subsidy Schemes in Different States of India

Uttar Pradesh

The state government of Uttar Pradesh announced a subsidy of Rs 15,000 per kW for the development of rooftop solar projects which will be provided to residential consumers. The state government has set a target of 10,700 MW for solar power, including 4,300 MW from rooftop solar projects, by 2022. UP's total installed solar power capacity was 1,095 MW in February with solar rooftop capacity of 146MW.

Kerala

In the state of Kerela, for group housing society projects and residential welfare associations, with a capacity up to 500 kW, the center will provide a subsidy of 20%. If a customer wishes to install the plant investing the whole amount, they can choose to avail up to 40% of plant cost as subsidy.

Read More

Madhya Pradesh

The government announced a 40% subsidy for up to 3 kW system for the state of Madhya Pradesh but it was difficult to get due to the unavailability of funds during the pandemic. It is available for residential homeowners who install on-grid solar systems. It is provided by the state Discom company after submitting the complete installation documents on their portal. After availing of the subsidy, a 3kW rooftop solar system in MP would cost you round Rs. 2.5 lakhs, only. In order to qualify for the subsidy, one must buy the solar panels that are manufactured in India only.

Read More

West Bengal

West Bengal is a pioneer in solar technology in India. Government-owned WEBEL was one of the first manufacturers of solar energy panels in the country. It is mandatory for all large housing societies in West Bengal, (having a total contract demand of more than 500 kW) to install solar rooftop systems to meet a minimum of 1.5% of their total electrical load by solar energy. The subsidies are applicable as per SECI guidelines.

Read More

Bihar

The government announced a 40% subsidy for up to 3 kW system for the state of Bihar but it was difficult to get funds due to the pandemic. The subsidy is available for residential homeowners who install on-grid solar systems. It is provided by the state discom company after submitting the complete installation documents on their portal. To avail the solar subsidies in Bihar, a potential customer needs to visit the BREDA website or they can also do so through the state DISCOM. The channel partners can also help in applying and claiming the subsidies. Also, to avail the rooftop solar subsidy in Bihar, the rooftop solar system should be a minimum of 1kW in size.

Haryana

To save the resources for the future generation of Haryana (Department of Renewal Energy) has started an electricity saving project called as Solar Rooftop Power Plant. Under this scheme, Solar System Plants are provided to every Residential, Institutional, Social Sector, Private, and Commercial & Industrial Sector across state. Haryana government launched solar subsidy of 30% of the benchmark cost for this sector. Besides, 70% subsidy in hill stations states like Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, and Jammu & Kashmir, and 90% subsidy is available for farmers on water pumping systems.
